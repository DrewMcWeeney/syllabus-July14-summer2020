Course Description
The purpose of the American Red Cross Lifeguarding course is to provide entry-level participants with the knowledge and skills to
recognize, respond to, prevent aquatic emergencies, and provide care for breathing, cardiac emergencies, injuries and sudden illnesses
until emergency medical services (EMS) take over. Participants who successfully complete this course receive American Red Cross
certification for Lifeguarding, First Aid, and CPR/AED valid for two years.


Course Policy
In order to obtain your initial lifeguarding certification, you have to successfully complete this course. In order to successfully complete this course, you must attend and stay for the duration of every class, complete and demonstrate at or above competency with both the course
skills and content, and pass two written exams and two water skill exams. You need an 80% score or better for each written exam, and
a 100% score for each in-water exam.

Course Goals
At the end of this course, you will be able to:

1. Describe and explain the components of the lifeguarding profession.
2. Describe and explain the methods and techniques in working with patrons.
3. Describe and explain the legal protocols [the laws and ethics] of lifeguarding.
4. Describe and explain best practices and procedures for injury prevention.
5. Describe and explain the techniques for effective surveillance.
6. Demonstrate all first aid, CPR, and AED skills.
7. Demonstrate all water rescue skills.

Disability and Condition Statement
Before participating in this course, I urge you to check with your healthcare professional because this course will require extremely
strenuous physical activity. If you have a medical condition or disability that might prevent you from participating in this course, or if you
have any questions about participating in this course, please contact me before the course begins.




Course Homework
This course will require you to complete assignments and further study on your own. Such further study and assignments are assigned
to you to gain a deeper understanding of the content we talk about in class. When we meet in person, you will get the breadth of the content
and when you work on your own, you will get the depth of the content. You must complete all assignments, competently, to qualify for certification.
